Look for these signs to indicate a rodent infestation.

  • Tracks

    Footprints and tail marks can be seen on dusty surfaces.

  • Droppings

    Droppings can be found where rodents travel.

  • Urine Stains

    Rodents constantly urinate because they do not have bladders like humans.

  • Gnaw Marks

    May be visible anywhere under the hood.

  • Nests

    Rodents commonly nest in the cabin air filter chamber, engine air filter, and on top of a warm engine.

  • Trap, Don't Poison!

    Use traps to fight back against your rodent problem. Do NOT use poisons in or around your vehicle.
    If you use poisons you may end up with a dead rat inside your engine compartment in such a location that costly assistance from a technician is required in order to remove the carcass. Removing the smell, should the rodent die inside an air duct or vent could prove even more difficult.

  • Use The Right Trap.

    We recommend using T-Rex mouse and rat traps. These traps are easy to set up and bait without risking a finger, and allow you to dispose of the trapped rodent without coming into direct contact with it. Both sizes are available for purchase from this site. Chose the T-Rex Rat Trap for your larger rats, or the T-Rex Mini for smaller mice.

  • Place Your Traps

    Rats and mice have poor vision and will usually run along side walls, pipes, shelves and other structures for guidance. Place your traps perpendicular to these objects, with the trigger end pointed inward. Place your traps in areas that show signs of frequent rodent activity.
